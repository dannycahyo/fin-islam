# Islamic Finance Overview

## Introduction

Islamic finance is a system of banking and financial activities that complies with **Shariah (Islamic law)** principles. It emphasizes ethical and socially responsible investing.

## Core Principles

### 1. Prohibition of Interest (Riba)

- Interest is strictly forbidden
- Money should not generate income by itself
- Focus on productive economic activity

### 2. Risk Sharing

- Profits and losses are shared equitably
- Encourages partnership and cooperation
- Promotes fair distribution of wealth

### 3. Asset-Backed Transactions

All financial dealings must be backed by real assets or services.

## Islamic Financial Products

| Product   | Description                |
| --------- | -------------------------- |
| Murabaha  | Cost-plus-profit sale      |
| Mudaraba  | Profit-sharing partnership |
| Musharaka | Joint venture              |
| Sukuk     | Islamic bonds              |
| Ijara     | Islamic lease              |

## Benefits

- Ethical investing
- Financial stability
- Social justice
- Economic development

## Conclusion

Islamic finance offers an alternative financial system that prioritizes ethical practices and social responsibility.
